🌿
Allulose
Rare sugar — acts like sugar, metabolizes like fiber
What it is
A rare naturally-occurring monosaccharide found in small amounts in figs and raisins. Tastes and bakes like sugar but is mostly excreted without being metabolized.
Why it matters
Provides the functional texture and caramelization of sugar in baking with approximately 0.4 kcal/g vs sugar's 4 kcal/g. Supports the fudgy brownie texture authentically.
Gut-Brain Benefit
Research suggests allulose may improve insulin sensitivity and has no negative effect on gut microbiota — unlike most sugar substitutes.

Clean Sweetener Philosophy

Why We Don't Use
Sugar Alcohols

Many low-carb snacks rely on erythritol and maltitol to hit their sweetness targets. These compounds are common gut irritants that can disrupt your microbiome — the opposite of what we're building toward.

We use monk fruit and allulose instead. Both provide sweetness without glycemic impact, without gut disruption, and without the bloating that sugar alcohols cause.

What We Avoid
✗Erythritol — common gut irritant
✗Maltitol — spikes blood sugar
✗Sorbitol — causes bloating
✗Sucralose — disrupts microbiome
✗Added cane sugar — glucose spike
What We Use
✓Monk Fruit — zero glycemic impact
✓Allulose — metabolized like fiber
✓Natural flavors — from real sources
✓Cacao — unsweetened, antioxidant-rich
✓Himalayan salt — enhances sweetness naturally
